Growth Junction · Design Direction Review

Three contrasting directions for the new growthjunction.ae. Each is a complete, self-contained HTML mockup. Open in any browser. No setup needed. Pick the direction that feels closest, not the one that is most finished.

Editorial Premium
Direction 01

Editorial Premium

Serif voice. Long-form. Reads like a journal of practice rather than a service brochure. Quiet authority.

  • Feels likeHBR, BetterUp, a senior practice that does not need to shout
  • Best forBuyers who care about depth, IP, and intellectual seriousness
  • RiskCan read aloof. Needs strong writing in production

Boutique Advisory
Direction 02

Boutique Advisory

Clean sans, structured grids, case-work led. Senior advisory tone with proof above the fold.

  • Feels likeA leaner Korn Ferry, a more confident Heidrick. Pragmatic, modern
  • Best forUAE CHRO and CEO buyers who scan for proof and structure
  • RiskMost "expected" of the three. Differentiation comes from copy and case work, not design

Human First
Direction 03

Human First

Warm palette, big faces, quotes from real practice, founder voice anchored above the fold. Coach-led, not corporate-led.

  • Feels likeA senior practitioner you would actually call when the week is hard
  • Best forPersonal credibility plays. Marshall Goldsmith methodology shown, not just claimed
  • RiskCloser to "coach" than "advisor". May feel light for enterprise procurement

How to use these. Open each in a browser. Spend 30 seconds, not 30 minutes. The first one that makes you say "yes, that is us" is the direction. Hybrid is fine. We can lift the hero of one onto the structure of another. The point of three contrasting directions is to surface preference, not to ship any of these as-is.
